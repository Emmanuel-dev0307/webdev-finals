Runny Nose

Description: A runny nose occurs when nasal tissues produce excess mucus.

Common Causes: Cold, flu, allergies, sinusitis.

Symptoms: Watery discharge, sneezing, nasal congestion.

Treatment: Rest, fluids, antihistamines, decongestants.

When to See a Doctor: If symptoms last more than 10 days or are accompanied by high fever.

Prevention: Wash hands frequently, avoid close contact with sick individuals.
