Dislocation

Description: A dislocation occurs when the bones in a joint are forced out of their normal position, commonly affecting the shoulder, elbow, finger, or knee.

Common Causes: Sudden impact or trauma, falls, sports injuries, accidents, or extreme twisting of a joint.

Symptoms: Visible deformity of the joint, intense pain, swelling, inability to move the affected area, and numbness or tingling around the joint.

Treatment: Immobilize the joint immediately, apply ice to reduce swelling, and seek emergency medical care — do not attempt to pop the joint back in place yourself.

When to See a Doctor: Always seek immediate medical attention for any suspected dislocation, especially if there is severe pain, visible deformity, or loss of sensation.

Prevention: Wear appropriate protective gear during sports, strengthen muscles around joints through exercise, and use proper techniques when lifting or playing contact sports.
