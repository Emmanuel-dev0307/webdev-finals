Male Fertility

Description: Male fertility refers to a man's biological ability to fertilize a female egg and initiate a pregnancy. This complex process depends on the production of healthy sperm, the ability to deliver it, and the proper functioning of the endocrine system.

THREE MAIN PILLARS OF FERTILITY:
  • Production: Healthy sperm must be produced by the testes. A healthy amount of testosterone and appropriate temperature control are necessary for this (the testes are located outside the body since sperm generation requires a colder environment).
  • Quantity and Quality: Sperm count (the quantity of sperm), morphology (the size and shape of the sperm), and motility (the sperm's capacity to travel toward the egg) all have a significant impact on fertility.
  • Delivery: The capacity to obtain and sustain an erection and ejaculation, as well as a clean channel (the vas deferens), are essential.
COMMON SYMPTOMS OF INFERTILITY:
  • Sexual Dysfunction: Difficulty with ejaculation, small volumes of fluid ejaculated, or reduced sexual desire.
  • Pain or Swelling: Pain, a lump, or swelling in the testicle area.
  • Hormonal Changes: Reduced facial or body hair or other signs of chromosomal or hormonal abnormality.
  • Small, Firm Testicles: This can sometimes indicate issues with sperm production.

Treatment: Fertility medications, surgery, assisted reproductive technology, lifestyle changes.

When To See A Doctor: If you and your partner have not been able to conceive after a year of consistent, unprotected sexual activity, or after six months if you are over 35, you should see a doctor or fertility expert. If you have a history of testicular injuries, surgery, or childhood diseases like the mumps, it is crucial to have a medical checkup as soon as possible since these conditions might affect your ability to produce sperm in the long run. A straightforward, non-invasive test called a semen analysis can be used by a physician to offer quick insight into the health of sperm. Since many reasons of male infertility, such infections or hormonal abnormalities, are quite curable once discovered, early intervention is essential.

Tips: Avoid excessive heat to groin, limit alcohol, quit smoking, manage stress, eat a nutritious diet.
