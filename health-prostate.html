Prostate

Description: Prostate is a small, walnut-sized gland that is part of the male reproductive system. It is located just below the bladder and in front of the rectum, surrounding the urethra (the tube that carries urine and semen out of the body). Its primary job is to produce prostatic fluid, which makes up a significant portion of semen. This fluid nourishes and protects sperm, ensuring they are mobile enough for fertilization.

COMMON PROSTATE CONDITIONS:
  • Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH): This is a non-cancerous enlargement of the prostate. Because the gland surrounds the urethra, an enlarged prostate can squeeze the tube, making it difficult to urinate.
  • Prostatitis: Inflammation or infection of the prostate, which can cause pain, fever, and urinary frequency. It can affect men of all ages.
  • Prostate Cancer: One of the most common types of cancer in men. It usually grows slowly and is highly treatable if caught early, though some forms can be aggressive.

Symptoms: Frequent urination, weak urine stream, blood in urine, pelvic discomfort, erectile issues.

Treatment: Medications, lifestyle changes, surgery, radiation or hormone therapy for cancer.

When To See A Doctor: If you have chronic urine changes, such as a weak stream, frequent overnight urination, or an unexpected urgency that interferes with your regular routine, you should speak with a healthcare provider. Even though these are frequently indicators of non-cancerous enlargement (BPH), a physical examination or a PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen) blood test are necessary to rule out more severe disorders. If you see blood in your urine or semen, have excruciating pelvic pain, or are unable to pee at all, you should get medical help right away since these "red flags" might be signs of an acute infection or advanced illness.

Prevention: Eat a healthy diet, exercise regularly, get regular PSA screenings after age 50.
