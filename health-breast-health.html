Breast Health

Description: Breast health refers to the overall well-being of breast tissue and the proactive monitoring of any changes through regular self-awareness and professional screenings. Maintaining breast health is essential for the early detection of conditions ranging from benign cysts and infections (like mastitis) to breast cancer.

COMMON SYMPTOMS YOU SHOULD MONITOR:
  • Lumps or Masses
  • Skin Changes
  • Nipple Changes
  • Discharge
  • Size/Shape Alterations
  • Persistent Pain

Self-Exam: Check monthly for lumps, changes in size or shape, skin dimpling, or unusual discharge.

Screening: Mammograms recommended annually for women over 40 or earlier with family history.

When To See A Doctor: Anytime you see a persistent alteration that differs from your "normal" baseline, you should consult a healthcare professional about breast health. If you find a new lump or thickening in the breast or underarm area, especially if it seems firm or set in place, you should be checked out right once. Visible skin abnormalities including inexplicable redness, orange peel-like dimpling, or a nipple that has abruptly turned inward are other serious warning signs.

Tips: Maintain healthy weight, limit alcohol, avoid smoking, exercise regularly.
