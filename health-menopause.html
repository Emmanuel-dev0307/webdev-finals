Menopause

Description: Menopause is the point in time that marks the end of your menstrual cycles. It is officially diagnosed after you have gone 12 consecutive months without a menstrual period. This natural biological process typically occurs in your 40s or 50s (the average age in the U.S. is 51) and signifies the end of your reproductive years as the ovaries stop producing high levels of estrogen and progesterone.

Common Causes: Natural aging, surgical removal of ovaries, chemotherapy or radiation.

Menopause Symptoms:
  • Irregular Periods
  • Hot Flashes
  • Night Sweats
  • Sleep Disturbances
  • Mood Changes
  • Vaginal Dryness
  • Physical Changes

Treatment: Hormone therapy, antidepressants, lifestyle changes, vaginal estrogen.

When To See A Doctor: The intensity of symptoms and the timing of bodily changes play a major role in determining whether to see a doctor about menopause. Even though it's a normal hormonal shift, you should consult a doctor if symptoms like hot flashes, sleeplessness, or mood swings start to interfere with your day-to-day activities. Hormone therapy, for example, can provide substantial relief. Most importantly, any vaginal bleeding that happens after menopause (one full year without a period) is a medical red flag that has to be evaluated right once in order to rule out dangerous underlying disorders. Additionally, seeing a doctor throughout this change is essential for preventative care, particularly to keep an eye on cardiovascular health and bone density, which become more dangerous as oestrogen levels drop.

Tips: Stay active, eat calcium-rich foods, avoid triggers like caffeine and spicy food.
