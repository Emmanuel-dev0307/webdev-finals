Headache

Description: A headache is a pain or discomfort in the head, scalp, or neck area, ranging from mild and dull to severe and throbbing.

Common Causes: Stress, dehydration, lack of sleep, eye strain, tension, sinus congestion, or underlying medical conditions.

Symptoms: Throbbing or pressure pain in the head, sensitivity to light or sound, nausea, neck stiffness, or pain behind the eyes.

Treatment: Rest in a quiet dark room, stay hydrated, apply a cold or warm compress, and take over-the-counter pain relievers such as ibuprofen or paracetamol.

When to See a Doctor: If headaches are sudden and severe, occur frequently, are accompanied by fever, confusion, vision changes, or do not respond to medication.

Prevention: Stay hydrated, maintain regular sleep schedules, manage stress, limit screen time, and avoid known headache triggers.
