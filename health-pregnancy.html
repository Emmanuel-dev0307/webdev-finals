Pregnancy

Description: Pregnancy is the biological process in which an offspring develops inside a person’s uterus over approximately 40 weeks (divided into three trimesters). It begins with conception—when a sperm fertilizes an egg—and concludes with childbirth.

Trimesters: First (weeks 1–12), Second (weeks 13–26), Third (weeks 27–40).

Symptoms:
  • Missed Period
  • Morning Sickness
  • Fatigue
  • Breast Changes
  • Frequent Urination
  • Food Aversion/Cravings
  • Mood swings

Care: Prenatal vitamins, regular checkups, balanced diet, avoid alcohol and smoking.

When to See a Doctor: Some people may experience implantation bleeding (light spotting) or mild cramping about 10 to 14 days after conception, which is often mistaken for a light period. If you suspect you are pregnant, the most accurate way to confirm is through a home pregnancy test or a blood test at a clinic.

Tips: Stay active with light exercise, get enough sleep, manage stress, attend all prenatal appointments.
