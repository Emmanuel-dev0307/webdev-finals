Blisters

Description: Blisters are small pockets of fluid that form on the upper layers of skin due to friction, burns, or infection.

Common Causes: Friction, burns, frostbite, chemical exposure, chickenpox, herpes.

Symptoms: Raised fluid-filled bubble on skin, redness, tenderness, pain around the area.

Treatment: Keep clean and dry, do not pop, use bandage to protect, antiviral if caused by infection.

When to See a Doctor: If blister is very large, shows signs of infection, or is caused by a burn or allergic reaction.

Prevention: Wear properly fitted shoes, use gloves during manual work, apply sunscreen.
