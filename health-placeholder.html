General Allergies

Description: Allergies occur when the immune system reacts to substances that are usually harmless, called allergens.

Common Causes: Dust, pollen, pet dander, certain foods, insect bites, medications, and mold.

Symptoms: Sneezing, itching, rashes, watery eyes, swelling, runny nose, coughing, or difficulty breathing.

Treatment: Avoid allergens, take antihistamines, use allergy medication, and seek emergency care for severe reactions.

When to See a Doctor: If symptoms become severe, breathing becomes difficult, or allergic reactions happen frequently.

Prevention: Avoid known allergens, keep surroundings clean, check food labels, and carry prescribed medication if needed.
